Congressional Report Reveals Billions in Unclaimed Senior Benefits

By Sarah Mitchell, Senior Affairs Correspondent
Published: 2 hours ago | Read Time: 3 minutes | Updated: 10:47 AM EST

A shocking new report from the Government Accountability Office has revealed that American seniors are missing out on an average of $4,871 per year in unclaimed benefits and programs.

WASHINGTON — Federal auditors discovered that nearly 78% of eligible seniors are not taking advantage of programs specifically designed to reduce their healthcare, prescription, and living costs. The findings come at a critical time as inflation continues to impact fixed-income retirees.

"Most seniors simply don't know these programs exist," explained Robert Chen, the lead auditor on the report. "We're talking about legitimate federal and state programs that are fully funded but underutilized."

Key Finding: The report identified 11 specific programs that most seniors have never heard of, including prescription assistance, property tax freezes, and utility subsidies. Eligibility often depends on age, not income.

The investigation began after Senator Patricia Williams received numerous complaints from constituents struggling with rising costs despite qualifying for assistance. "It's unconscionable that these programs exist but aren't being properly communicated to our senior population," Williams stated at yesterday's press conference.

Among the most overlooked benefits are the Medicare Extra Help program, which can save up to $5,100 annually on prescriptions, and state-specific property tax exemptions that can reduce housing costs by 50% or more for qualifying seniors.

The report emphasizes that many of these benefits have December 31st deadlines for 2025 enrollment, creating urgency for seniors to review their eligibility immediately.
