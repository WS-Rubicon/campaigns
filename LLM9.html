We Ranked 5 Options From Good to Best...

5

Online-Only Courses

★★★☆☆ 3.0

Convenient and cheap, online-only courses let you complete training from your couch. But there's a catch: many states don't accept them, and you miss the hands-on practice that actually matters.

Pros

  • Lowest cost ($30-80)
  • Complete on your schedule
  • Good for review/refreshers

Cons

  • Limited state acceptance
  • No hands-on training
  • You're on your own for practice
4

Local Gun Shop Classes

★★★☆☆ 3.4

Many gun shops offer basic CCW classes, often as a way to sell more gear. Quality varies wildly — some are excellent, others are just checking boxes.

Pros

  • Usually includes range time
  • Local and convenient
  • Often discounts on gear

Cons

  • Instructor quality varies
  • May focus more on sales
  • Limited curriculum depth
3

NRA Basic Pistol Course

★★★★☆ 4.0

The NRA's standardized curriculum is widely recognized and covers fundamentals well. A solid choice, though it's more focused on general pistol skills than CCW-specific scenarios.

Pros

  • Nationally recognized
  • Standardized quality
  • Good foundation for beginners

Cons

  • Not CCW-specific
  • Limited legal coverage
  • Higher cost ($150-250)
2

USCCA Concealed Carry Class

★★★★☆ 4.3

The USCCA offers solid training with a focus on self-defense law. Their courses are well-structured, though they'll definitely try to sell you a membership.

Pros

  • Strong legal education
  • Professional instructors
  • Good support resources

Cons

  • Membership upsell pressure
  • Higher price point
  • Limited locations
1
★ Editor's Choice

State-Certified CCW Permit Class

★★★★★ 4.9

After reviewing dozens of options, state-certified CCW classes consistently came out on top. They're specifically designed for permit requirements, taught by certified instructors, and — here's the key — they're recognized in 38+ states through reciprocity agreements.

You get everything in one package: legal knowledge specific to concealed carry, hands-on range training, situational awareness scenarios, and the documentation you need to apply for your permit same-day. No fluff, no upsells — just the training that actually counts.

Pros

  • 38+ state reciprocity
  • Complete in one day
  • CCW-specific curriculum
  • Includes range qualification
  • Competitive pricing ($99-149)

Cons

  • Requires in-person attendance
  • Class availability varies
