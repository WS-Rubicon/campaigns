Top 5 Ways Homeowners Are Slashing Bathroom Remodel Costs in 2026

We reviewed the most popular approaches — from big-box stores to contractor networks — to find which actually delivers the biggest savings.

By Home Improvement Desk | 3 min read

If you've been putting off a bathroom remodel because of cost, you're not alone. The average bathroom renovation runs $15,000-$25,000 — and that's before the inevitable surprises. But savvy homeowners are discovering ways to cut that number significantly. We ranked the top 5 approaches.

The Rankings

#5

DIY From YouTube Popular

Sure, you'll save on labor. But unless you're already handy, you're likely to make costly mistakes. Plumbing errors alone can run $2,000+ to fix. Best for cosmetic updates only — paint, hardware, mirrors.

#4

Big-Box Store Installation Home Depot / Lowes

Convenient, but you're paying for that convenience. These programs typically run 15-30% higher than independent contractors. The installers are subcontractors anyway — you're just paying a middleman markup.

#3

Referral From Friends/Family Word of Mouth

Better than going in blind, but you're limited to one quote. Without competition, there's no incentive for the contractor to sharpen their pencil. You might get quality work, but you won't get the best price.

#2

Online Lead Services Angi / Thumbtack

Gets you multiple quotes, which is good. But vetting varies wildly. Some contractors pay to be listed without real quality checks. You'll spend hours sorting through responses and still might not find the right fit.

#1
★ Editor's Pick

Pre-Vetted Contractor Matching

Here's why this approach is dominating in 2026: You get multiple quotes from competing contractors who are actually vetted (licensed, insured, background-checked). The competition drives prices down. The pre-screening ensures quality stays up.

Homeowners report savings of $3,000-$8,000 compared to going it alone. The best services include 5-year warranties and $0 down financing options.
